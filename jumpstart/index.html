Taiwan is an island nation located in East Asia, off the southeastern coast of China. It has a population of over 23 million people and covers an area of approximately 36,000 square kilometers. The capital city is Taipei, and the official language is Mandarin, but other languages such as Taiwanese, Hakka, and indigenous languages are also spoken.

Taiwan has a vibrant economy and is one of the Four Asian Tigers, known for its rapid industrialization and economic growth. It is home to many well-known technology companies such as Acer, Asus, HTC, and Foxconn. The country is also a major producer of semiconductors and electronic components.
